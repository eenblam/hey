About hey

Hey is a personal relationship manager, kinda like Monica, but... a lot smaller. Think of it as a focused tool for managing your contacts - and when you last contacted them - without social media.

Overview

To use hey, you just list your friends and put them into groups. Each group represents a cadence for how often you want to touch base with its friends, for example "every two days" or "once a week."

Hey will help you remember when to follow-up with folks when it's time to.

Getting started

Once you've signed up, add some people you care about staying in touch with to your friends list. Hey has already created a few default groups for you, so you can get straight to adding friends to them:

  • Besties: every two days
  • Close friends: every week
  • Friends: every month (28 days)

Don't worry - you can rename groups, add more, and change their cadences on your groups page.

Once you have some friends in your list, hey will show you which are due for contact on your home page. Here, you can log the date you were last in touch with a friend, whether that was a text, call, hangout, dinner, whatever. You can use the "Yesterday" and "Today" buttons for convenience, or set a specific date using the datepicker widget.

You can also set a status for each friend, which could be used a number of different ways. This might be something you need to follow up on: a birthday coming up, a job interview to ask about, a hard conversation to have. It could also be used more loosely for setting intentions with that friend: "hit the gym together," "out of town til May," "try to hang more," etc.

Regardless, be sure to hit "Save" whenever you're done updating information!

Setting your timezone

When hey decides which friends are overdue for contact, it uses UTC+0000 (GMT) to decide the current day. This can be confusing if you're in a different timezone, so it's helpful to tell hey about yours on your account page for the best experience.

To find out your UTC timezone offset, you can visit timeanddate.com at this link to search for your current location. Look for the "Current Offset" field. For example, San Francisco, CA, is UTC-7, so you would set your timezone to -7.

Note that your timezone will change if your location observes Daylight Savings Time.
